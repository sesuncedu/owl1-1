Abstract

Entire community of OWL users, developers, and interested parties is invited to sent their comments through the W3C mailing list. These issues will be logged on this page and will be discussed during the pending standardization process.

Status of this Document

This is a editor's draft, for comment by the OWL community.

This document has been produced for approval of the ad-hoc OWL community. Comments, both from within and without the community, are welcome. Although the development of OWL 1.1 is not a W3C activity, public comments should be sent to the public-owl-dev@w3.org list to ensure widest visibility.

    RDF Mapping maps disjoint properties to owl:disjointWith, which may cause confusion (esp. with punning).
  • Annotation values are restricted to strings (that is, are more restricted than in OWL DL)
  • The current OWL 1.1 specification only allows one facet restriction per data range. This makes it cumbersome (and unintuitive) to specify ranges such as integer between 30 and 50. t should be possible to specify multiple facet restrictions for a data range.
  • Ontology consistency, concept satisfiability and subsumption are known to be EXPTIME-Complete for HORN-SHIQ
  • Entity annotations are currently allowed only in the entity declaration axioms. This makes it impossible to annotate an entity without declaring it. A potential correction to this problem would be to separate the entity declaration from the entity annotation axioms.
  • OWL 1.1 seems to provide for antisymmetric properties, but, by their semantics, the properties are asymmetric.
